Exact limits of certain recursively defined monotone sequenes

This article was originally made for multiple exercises in the the monotone convergence and infinite series exercises in my analysis book that is in this page. However this property seems pretty useful in general.

The sequences we will concern ourselves with are recursively defined convergent sequences $(x_n)$ such that there exists an open interval $A$ where if the starting point of the sequence $x_1$ is in $A$, then $(x_n)$ is strictly increasing and is always contained within $A$. Also if $f(x)$ is the function in the recursive definition of $(x_n)$, therefore $x_{n+1} = f(x)$, then $f(x)$ is 1-1 and continuous in $A$. Another important part in the investigation of such sequences is the upper bound of all such sequences which will be defined as $M = \sup A$. It will later be proven that in this case the sequence converges to $M$.
