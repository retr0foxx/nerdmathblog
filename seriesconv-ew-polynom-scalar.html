The sum of a strictly decreasing sequence converging to zero with it's terms individually multiplied by a polynomial in terms of the nth term.

Suppose $(x_n)$ is a strictly decreasing sequence which converges to zero and $\sum_{i=1}^{\infty} x_i = a$ exists and $p(x) = \sum_{i=0}^{d} c_i x^{i - 1}$ is a polynomial with degree $d$. Prove that $\sum_{i=1}^{\infty} p(i) x_i$ converges regardless of what $p(x)$ is.

One important tool that will be used in this proof is about nested sums.
