Combinatorics and "nested sums"

Define $S(d, m)$ to be a "nested sum" of degree $d$ with $m$ terms where $S(1, m) = \sum_{i=1}^m i$ and $S(d, m) = \sum_{i=1}^m S(d - 1, m - i + 1)$. For example, $S(2, m) = (1) + (1 + 2) + ... + (1 + 2 + ... + m)$.

Define $C(n, k)$ to be n choose k which is equivalent to $C(n, k) = \sum_{i=1}^{n - k + 1} C(n - i, k - 1)$ but then we also know that $C(n, k) = \frac{n!}{k!(n - k)!}$ and we will talk about the relation of $C$ with $S$. As of writing this, the goal is to prove that $S(d, x)$ with $d$ being locked as a function of $x$ is equal to a polynomial with degree $d + 1$.
